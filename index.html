Profile

I qualified from Guys and St Thomas' Hospital in 1997 and then worked in a number of units across the south of England, completing my higher surgical training in the South West Thames region. I undertook my endovascular fellowship back at St Thomas' Hospital and then the Royal Sussex County Hospital, Brighton.

I am a substantive NHS consultant surgeon at Frimley Park NHS Hospital with a specialist interest in endovascular techniques and interventional radiology. I jointly lead for vascular simulator training and teach on a number of courses about vascular and general surgery (including the Registrar Ready Course, St Thomasâ€™ MRCS course and London post-graduate EVAR course). I am the the vascular lead for the diabetic foot serviceat Royal Surrey, Guildford Hospital and lead on a new stent graft system called Nellix, used in the treatment of infra-renal and more complex aneurysms.

I maintain an active research interest and I am the principal investigator at Frimley Park for the 3D image registration mapping project for aortic aneurysm repair. I have published in a number of peer reviewed journals on general surgical and vascular topics and presented at a number of National and International meetings.

I am a director of a medical software company (Clinical Software Solutions) specialising in developing healthcare software and mobile applications.
