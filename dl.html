Flare and Thread Vein Treatments

Within the relaxing environment of the Amida Spa Farnham we offer treatment for flare / thread veins.

What are flare veins

Flare veins (also known as thread or broken veins) are tiny surface veins that can appear blue, red or pale green. They can be on any part of the body but are most commonly treated on the legs and face.

We offer treatment with Micro Sclerotherapy.

Micro Sclerotherapy

Sclerotherapy involves injecting a small volume of a irritant liquid into affected veins. The liquid acts upon the lining of the vein to cause it to seal shut, eliminating the visibility of the vein. Sclerotherapy can be quickly performed at our clinic and no anaesthesia is required.

What to expect

When injected, the vein disappears, but then almost immediately becomes red and inflamed. The leg can ache after treatment and this is a consequence of the irritant liquid working. The inflammation settles over a period of weeks and should settle completely by six weeks.

Other risks include: allergic reactions, bruising, small skin sores or ulcers, darkening of the skin.

There are limits to the area that can be treated in each sitting and sometimes an area will need to be treated more than once.
